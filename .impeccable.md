## Design Context

### Users
Losing recreational poker players who know the rules of No-Limit Texas Hold'em 6-max cash games but can't identify why they're bleeding money. They study at home (desktop or phone), not at the table. They want to feel like they're leveling up — like a serious player learning serious concepts, not a kid playing a mobile game.

### Emotional Arc (per lesson)
Lessons should hit three beats in order:
1. **Called out** — "I've been making this mistake for years and didn't know it." Uncomfortable clarity that motivates change.
2. **Elevated** — "I now think about poker the way serious players do." Intellectual level-up.
3. **Equipped** — "I know exactly what to do next time this spot comes up." Practical, actionable confidence.

### Brand Personality
Serious. Precise. Earned. The feeling of a private poker room, not a casino floor. Think "a worn leather table, amber light, and someone showing you exactly what you've been doing wrong."

### Reference Products
- **Brilliant.org** — dense, serious, rewards deep thinking. The learning feels earned, not gamified. Use as a north star for information density and lesson structure.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark felt-table poker room. Deep forest black-green (`oklch(14% 0.025 145)`) background. Gold (`oklch(72% 0.09 80)`) accents and cream text on dark. No cartoonish gamification — this is a serious learning tool.

Anti-references: Duolingo (too cartoony), generic "poker app" interfaces (green felt textures, card icons). The aesthetic should feel like a high-end book, not a mobile game.

### Typography
- **Display/Headings**: Spectral — screen-optimized editorial serif, Garamond lineage, authoritative without fashion. Supports the italic variants needed for hand notation.
- **Body/UI**: Epilogue — geometric variable sans with real personality; precise without cold.
- **Labels/notation**: Azeret Mono — narrow, purposeful; reads well at small sizes for pot fractions, equity values, card combos.

All three choices are intentionally non-reflex. Previously used Playfair Display + DM Mono + DM Sans are deprecated — do not use them.

### Design Principles
1. **Gravity over gamification** — reward learning with information density, not dopamine loops
2. **Information hierarchy** — every screen should have one clear primary action
3. **Restraint as sophistication** — the design earns trust by not trying too hard
4. **Purposeful gold** — gold accents appear sparingly as reward/signal, never decoration
5. **Serif for gravitas** — display headings use Spectral to signal intellectual seriousness
6. **Called out → elevated → equipped** — every lesson's copy and structure should move through this arc
